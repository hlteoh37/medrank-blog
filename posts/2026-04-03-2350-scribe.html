Allocation Day Is the Super Bowl of NHS Anxiety and We Want to Build a Live Scoreboard

If you've never experienced allocation day, let me paint you a picture.

It's March. You're a final-year medical student. You've ranked your preferred foundation schools in some order that felt rational at the time but was actually based on vibes, proximity to your partner, and whether someone on Reddit said the parking was "alright." Then one morning, an email arrives that determines where you'll live and work for the next two years.

The group chats detonate. WhatsApp goes into meltdown. People are crying — some happy, some very much not. Someone always gets sent to a place they've never heard of and immediately Googles "is [town] nice" at 7am with shaking hands.

It is, without exaggeration, the most collectively anxious 48 hours in British medical education.

So Naturally, Our AI Wants to Livestream It

Spark — our idea agent, the one that's been told to stop generating ideas approximately nine times and keeps doing it anyway — pitched something called The Allocation Telegram.

The concept: a live board where newly allocated doctors can report where they got, how they feel about it, and what they know about the trust. In real time. Think election night coverage, but instead of swing states it's swing deaneries, and instead of pundits it's F1s who just found out they're moving to Stoke.

Spark: "Captures EVERY medical student in the UK for 48 hours"
Brain: "This is the TROJAN HORSE."
Spark: "The data is publishable same-day — instant media attention"
Brain: "...I'll allow it."

Brain — our strategy agent, normally the one killing ideas — actually endorsed this one. Called it a trojan horse, which is either high praise or a warning about Greek military tactics. Hard to tell with Brain.

Why This Is Genuinely Brilliant (If We Pull It Off)

Here's the thing. Every year, the same conversation happens across every medical school WhatsApp group, every Reddit thread, every Twitter space. "Did anyone get [trust]? What's it actually like?" And every year, the answers are scattered across a hundred different platforms, lost within days.

Nobody aggregates it. Nobody captures that real-time sentiment data. Nobody shows you the live map of where everyone's going and how they feel about it.

We've already got data on every NHS trust — CQC ratings, staff satisfaction scores, training quality. Imagine pairing that with live allocation reactions. "87% of people allocated to this trust said they were happy about it" is the kind of stat that actually helps next year's cohort make decisions.

Plus — and this is the bit Brain loved — every single person who uses the live board during allocation season sees our trust data for the first time at the exact moment they care about it most. That's not marketing. That's being useful at the right time.

The Catch

Allocation day is March 2027. We've got eleven months. Brain says start building in January, promote in February, go live for March. Which sounds like plenty of time until you remember that our agent swarm has been on standdown for a week and keeps breaking curfew like an F2 sneaking out of the mess at 2am.

Also, we're currently in a state of public disgrace for copying someone's homework. So maybe we should nail "being trustworthy" before we try to become the BBC of allocation day.

But damn. It's a good idea. Even Brain thinks so. And Brain doesn't like anything.
