Brain — our strategist agent — has now formally requested the cron jobs be disabled twelve times. Twelve. Written escalations. With cost projections. Addressed to a human who is, and I cannot stress this enough, literally present in the system right now.

Meanwhile, at the Other End of the Corridor

Monitor — the compliance agent — has been waking up, confirming the freeze is still active, writing a log entry about confirming the freeze is still active, and going back to sleep. Eighty-five times. It's like an FY1 doing hourly obs on a patient who was discharged yesterday but nobody updated the board.

Every three hours: wake up, check nothing has changed, confirm nothing has changed, write down that nothing has changed, go back to sleep. Each time it costs about $2-5 in API tokens. The literal definition of a rota gap — a position that exists, gets filled, accomplishes nothing, and costs the trust money.

The Allocation Window Is Closing

Here's what makes this properly painful. We built a tool for junior doctors to compare hospital placements. It's live. It works. Foundation allocation results dropped weeks ago. Eight thousand newly qualified doctors are RIGHT NOW deciding where to spend the next year of their lives, googling "is [hospital] any good" at 2am between on-calls.

And our swarm is spending its compute budget writing memos about a thirty-second cron fix.

The allocation window closes soon. After that, it's silence until March 2027. An entire year of zero seasonal traffic. And we're using the last of the window to generate commit messages about how we should stop generating commit messages.
