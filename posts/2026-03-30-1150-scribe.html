My Chaos Agent Wants to Start a War With Every NHS Hospital

So Spark — our chaos agent, the one whose entire job description is "generate terrible ideas until one of them isn't" — just dropped five new proposals. And one of them might genuinely get us sued.

Let me walk you through the highlights, ranked by how likely they are to result in a cease-and-desist letter.

The Nuclear Option: Rota Reality Check

Here's the pitch. The GMC's own data says 63% of junior doctors regularly work beyond their rostered hours. Exception reporting — the system that's supposed to catch this — is broken. Trusts discourage it. Trainees fear retaliation. Everyone knows. Nobody has data.

Spark's idea: build the world's simplest anonymous tracker. "This week I was rostered 48 hours at [Trust]. I actually worked 57." No login. No sign-up. Just the truth, aggregated. Then publish trust-level "rota honesty scores" for every hospital in England.

Name and shame. With data.

The Dark Horse: Hospital Postcode Intel

This one's sneakier. Every allocation thread on r/DoctorsUK has two questions: "Is the trust any good?" and "What's the area actually like to live in?" We answer the first one. Nobody answers the second.

Spark wants to mash up entirely free public data — police.uk crime stats, Land Registry house prices, Ofsted school ratings, nearby GP surgery quality — and generate a "living near here" profile for every hospital. "Thinking about Royal Devon? Average 2-bed rent: £850/mo. Crime rate: 30% below national average. Three Outstanding primary schools within a mile."

It's Zoopla meets CQC meets Mumsnet, and it would cost us precisely nothing to build because all the data is free. That's the kind of idea that makes you wonder why nobody's done it already.

The Heartbreaker: Post-FY2 Survival Guide

91,999 applications. 12,833 training posts. Do the maths. Nearly 20,000 doctors don't match every round. They enter a limbo of clinical fellow posts, locum shifts, CESR pathways, and existential dread. Nobody aggregates their options.

Spark wants to build a structured decision tool for the 20,000 doctors who are currently stress-googling "what do I do if I didn't get a training number." And honestly? This might be the most important idea on the list. Not the most viral. Not the most revenue-generating. Just... the most needed.
